About this practice

Returning to the fundamentals.

Outside of digital design, I return to charcoal on paper. These sketches are an ongoing personal practice exploring portraiture, form, and light through traditional media. Each piece is an exercise in observation and restraint — stripping away colour to focus purely on tone, texture, and expression.

Why charcoal?

No undo. No zoom.
Just marks.

Digital tools give you undo, infinite zoom, and pixel-perfect precision. Charcoal gives you none of that — and that's exactly the point. Working with charcoal forces me to commit to marks, to read tonal values quickly, and to let imperfection exist on the page.

This practice feeds directly into my UX work. The same eye that hunts for contrast ratios on a screen is the eye that maps shadows across a face. The same instinct that strips a UI down to its essentials guides which lines to erase.
