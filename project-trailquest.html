Overview

Hike Smarter, Explore Safer.

TrailQuest is a trail-discovery platform designed for hikers who want more than just a map. It connects users with real-time trail conditions, community hazard reports, and organised trip management solving the information gaps that put hikers at risk every day.

The Problem

Hikers are going in blind.

Research revealed that 40% of hikers encounter unexpected hazards on trails — fallen trees, flooded paths, sudden closures — that they had no way of knowing about in advance. The root cause is a persistent information gap: existing apps offer static, outdated data that doesn't reflect real-world conditions. Hikers show up unprepared because the tools they rely on haven't kept pace with the environment.

The problem doesn't stop with individual hikers. Trail organisers and rangers struggle to communicate closures and safety alerts quickly enough to reach people already on their way. This operational misalignment creates a disconnect between the people managing trails and the people using them — one that no existing platform had effectively bridged.

The Solution

Three features that change the experience.

TrailQuest addresses each pain point with purpose-built features. Real-time alerts pull live updates from rangers and verified community members so users are notified of closures, hazards, and changes before they leave the house. A quick hazard reporting tool lets any hiker flag a danger in seconds, feeding information back into the system and keeping the data current for everyone who follows.

Trip management ties it all together — letting users plan their hike, register for organised trips, access offline maps, and review safety guidelines in one place. The goal was to remove friction at every step, so that staying safe on the trail feels effortless rather than like extra work.

Key Screens

A closer look at the experience.

01

Homepage & Trail Discovery

The homepage leads with a powerful search and filter system letting users find trails by water sources, difficulty, and facilities. A full-bleed hero image sets the mood, while the persistent "Report Hazard" button ensures safety tools are always within reach. Curated hiking trip cards surface organised experiences below the fold.

Search & Filter Trail Cards Hazard CTA
02

Trail Detail Page

Each trail page surfaces the most critical data upfront — distance, elevation gain, estimated time, and daily price — so users can make a confident decision before committing. A tabbed layout cleanly separates Overview, Conditions, Reviews, and Trail Updates, while essential visitor info covers accessibility, features, and permitted activities at a glance.

Trail Metrics Tabbed Nav Visitor Info
03

Reviews Page

The Reviews tab builds trust through community voices. A star rating breakdown gives users a quick data snapshot, while detailed written reviews with photos tell the real story of the trail. Users can contribute their own experience, making the platform's data richer and more reliable for every hiker who follows.

Reflection

What this project taught me.

TrailQuest pushed me to think beyond visual aesthetics and focus on how information flows through an entire system — connecting hikers, organisers, and rangers in real time. The biggest design challenge was balancing information density with clarity: trail detail pages carry a lot of data, and every hierarchy decision directly affects how quickly a user can assess their safety.

I also learned how much the tone of a product matters. The dark green palette and photography-forward layouts weren't just aesthetic choices — they were deliberate signals that communicated trust, nature, and seriousness of purpose before a single word was read.
