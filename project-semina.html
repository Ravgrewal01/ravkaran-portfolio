Overview

Plant care made simple, guided, and smart.

Semina, from the Latin word for "seeds", is a mobile plant care app designed for people who love plants but struggle to keep them alive. It provides personalised care guidance, real-time health monitoring, and smart automation, turning the guesswork of plant ownership into a confident, daily habit.

The Problem

Most people struggle to keep plants alive.

The problem isn't a lack of care — it's a lack of knowledge. Most people don't know the right watering amount, ideal sunlight placement, or how to read their plant's warning signs. They overwater, underwater, or simply forget. Without guidance, even well-intentioned plant owners watch their plants decline and give up.

Monitoring environmental conditions like soil moisture and temperature adds another layer of complexity that most people aren't equipped to handle without the right tools. Existing apps offer generic advice that doesn't account for specific species, local climate, or individual plant health leaving users with tips that feel irrelevant to their actual situation.

The Solution

Four features that make plant care manageable.

Semina addresses the root causes of plant failure with purpose-built features. Manage Plants gives users a personalised garden — each plant tracked with health status, care history, and tailored guidance. Health Scan lets users identify issues in real time: soil moisture levels, pesticide risk alerts, and favoured conditions are surfaced directly on the plant detail page.

AR Space Mapping helps users find the right spot in their home for each plant based on light and temperature requirements. The LogBook keeps a running record of care activities, while Device Sensor Connectivity pairs with physical soil sensors over Bluetooth — giving users real data on moisture and conditions without any guesswork. Together, these features make plant care easier, more manageable, and more successful for everyone.

Key Screens

A closer look at the experience.

01

Home — My Garden

The home screen acts as a personal garden dashboard greeting users by name, surfacing recently added plants with health status indicators, listing today's care tasks, and showing trending seasonal plants in their region. A search bar and quick-add button keep the experience fast and frictionless.

Plant Status Daily Tasks Trending Plants
02

Plant Detail & Health Scan

Each plant's detail page surfaces everything a user needs to care for it confidently species description, health status, soil moisture levels, risk alerts for pesticide use, favoured conditions, and a map showing where the plant is commonly found. A real-time health indicator tells users exactly when to water.

Health Status Risk Alerts Care Conditions
03

Watering Schedule

The watering schedule gives users a simple, visual view of the next five watering days — each shown as a water-drop capsule with the amount in millilitres. A prominent "Watering" action button makes it easy to log care with one tap, and the schedule auto-updates based on the plant's health data and sensor readings.

Smart Scheduling Auto-Update One-Tap Logging
04

Device Sensor Connectivity

Semina pairs with physical soil sensors over Bluetooth to bring real environmental data into the app. The device pairing screen visualises the connection process clearly, and alerts users instantly if a sensor disconnects — with a "Reconnect here" prompt to restore the link. This bridges the gap between digital guidance and real-world conditions.

Bluetooth Pairing Soil Moisture Live Sensor Data
Reflection

What this project taught me.

Semina pushed me to design for users who are genuinely anxious about getting things wrong plant owners who've failed before and don't trust themselves. That meant every piece of information on screen needed to feel reassuring rather than overwhelming. The health indicators, alert messages, and care tips were all written and designed to build confidence, not add pressure.

Integrating the hardware the Bluetooth soil sensor, into a mobile design flow was a new challenge. The pairing screen had to communicate a technical process in a way that felt approachable and calm, even when things go wrong (like a failed connection). That tension between technical reality and user emotion shaped a lot of the design decisions throughout the project.
